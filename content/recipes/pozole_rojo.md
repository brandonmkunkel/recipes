## Ingredients

**For the Pork and Broth:**

- 3 pounds boneless pork shoulder, trimmed of excess fat and cut into 2-inch cubes
- 1 tablespoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt, plus more for seasoning; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 pound pig’s feet (optional)
- 1 whole white onion, peeled, ends trimmed (8 ounces; 227g)
- 5 medium cloves garlic, peeled and left whole
- 2 bay leaves
- Five 15-ounce cans white hominy (75 ounces total weight), drained and rinsed

**For the Chile Sauce:**

- 5 guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 4 California chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 1/2 white onion, trimmed, halved, and roughly chopped (4 ounces; 113g)
- 5 medium cloves garlic, roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon powder such as Knorr brand or substitute with 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

**For Serving:**

- Diced white onion
- Thinly sliced radish
- Thinly sliced cabbage
- Cubed avocado
- Dried Mexican oregano
- Crema
- Tostadas or chicharrón
- Lime wedges

## Instructions

**1. Prepare the Pork and Broth:**

1. Generously season pork shoulder all over with salt and pepper.
2. In a large stockpot or large Dutch oven, add the pork shoulder, pig’s feet, if using, onion, garlic, and bay leaves. Add 2 1/2 quarts of water and 1 tablespoon salt.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to low and cook, covered, adjusting heat as needed and occasionally skimming any scum and fat that rises to the surface, until a richly flavored broth is created and pork is just beginning to turn tender, about 1 hour.

**2. Make the Chile Sauce:**

1. Meanwhile, in a high-walled stainless-steel skillet, toast chiles over medium-low heat until aromatic, turning occasionally, about 2 minutes. Add 2 cups of water, bring to a boil, then immediately turn off heat and soak chiles until softened, about 5 minutes.
2. In a blender, blend the chiles with their soaking water, garlic, onion, black peppercorns, cumin seeds, and chicken bouillon powder until smooth, about 1 minute. Season with salt to taste.

**3. Simmer and Finish:**

1. After the pork has cooked for 1 hour in step 1, add the hominy and pour the blended chile sauce into the pork and broth mixture through a fine mesh strainer.
2. Continue to cook on low heat, covered, adjusting heat as needed to maintain a gentle simmer until pork is fork-tender (broth yield should be about 2 1/2 quarts), about 2 more hours.
3. Discard pig’s feet, onion, and bay leaves. Use a ladle to skim off any excess fat, if needed. Season to taste with salt.

**4. Serve:**

1. Ladle into individual serving bowls and serve with toppings (onion, radish, cabbage, avocado, oregano, and lime juice) on the side to be added at the table. Tostadas can be shattered into the soup or slathered in crema and eaten on the side.

## Tips

- Canned hominy can be found in the Mexican aisle of most well-stocked supermarkets.
- This recipe can easily be doubled; if doubling, you will need a very large stockpot, or need to cook in 2 separate batches.
- The chile sauce can be made ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
- Pozole can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days or frozen for up to 3 months.

## Modifications

- **Pork Alternatives**: You can use beef (like chuck roast or falda) or chicken instead of pork shoulder.
- **Pig's Feet Substitution**: If you cannot find or prefer not to use pig's feet, you can omit them (though the broth will be less rich/collagen-heavy) or substitute them with _espinazo de puerco_ (pork backbone).
- **Spicier Broth**: Add a handful of _chiles de árbol_ to the chile blend for extra heat.
- **Savory Twist**: Drizzle in a small splash of fish sauce for added savory/umami depth.
- **Alternative Herbs**: Add some fresh marjoram and oregano to the basic stock along with the bay leaves.
